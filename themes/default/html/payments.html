Payments are designed to track distributions or other income that would not otherwise be accounted for in this asset. An example would be a cash dividend recieved to an account other than the this asset.

A payment should not be added if its value is already accounted for in the asset's total value; For example, an accumulation fund, or interest on a savings account (if the interest is paid into the same account)
